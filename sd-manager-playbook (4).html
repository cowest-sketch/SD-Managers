Daily Priorities

The non-negotiables every manager should check each working day. Most take under 15 minutes total — the goal is early detection, not micromanagement.

⚡ Leverage 4 activities
Check Team Activity Metrics
Leverage
"Activity deviations caught on day 1 are fixable. Caught on day 10, they're a pipeline problem."
Why this matters
A quick daily scan lets you coach in real time, not in crisis mode. Patterns become visible early so coaching conversations are data-driven, not reactive.
How to do it ▼
  • Open go/sdperf — scan for any rep with activity below their daily target
  • Open go/sdfunnel — check for drop-offs in MQL → SAO conversion
  • Flag any rep consistently below trend; book a short check-in, don't wait for their 1:1
  • Note patterns (specific days, specific reps) for deeper weekly coaching conversations
go/sdperf go/sdfunnel
Monitor Lead Routing & MQL Flow
Leverage
"If leads aren't flowing, nothing else matters."
Why this matters
MQL routing issues silently kill pipeline. A stuck or misrouted lead today is a missed SAO this quarter. Two minutes of checking prevents hours of firefighting later.
How to do it ▼
  • Confirm new MQLs have been routed and assigned correctly to your reps
  • Check that no inbound leads are sitting unworked past 24 hours
  • If routing is down or delayed, escalate immediately — don't wait for end of day
  • Verify Sales Navigator access is active for any new or returning reps
Sales Navigator go/sdfunnel
Address DA Tickets & Tool Access Issues
Leverage
"Your job is to clear blockers — not be one."
Why this matters
Unresolved tool access issues directly block rep productivity. Manager presence alone accelerates resolution — a manager-cc'd email on a DA ticket often gets resolved in minutes, not days.
How to do it ▼
  • Check for any open DA (Data Assurance) tickets from your team
  • If SLA is at risk, escalate via Leads_DataAssurance@linkedin.com
  • Coach reps to CC you on escalations so you can jump in quickly when needed
  • Once reps know the process, encourage them to self-manage — you stay in copy
  • Target: Sales Navigator access within 4 business days for all new hires
Leads_DataAssurance@linkedin.com
Scan Aging Pipeline Opportunities
Leverage
"Pipeline hygiene is a daily habit, not a quarterly cleanup."
Why this matters
Aging opportunities are deal slippage risk. Proactive multithreading is most effective when an opportunity is still warm — not after it's been silent for three weeks.
How to do it ▼
  • Scan for open opportunities static for 7+ days — flag for rep action
  • Identify any approaching quarter-end without meaningful movement
  • Work with reps to layer in multithreading on key accounts where appropriate
  • Coach reps to ask: "What will close, when will it close, and why?" — forecasting is a learnable skill

Daily Check-ins for Ramping SDs
Talent
"Speed to first call is the best predictor of ramp success. Goal: Day 7."
Why this matters
The first 90 days set the trajectory. A brief daily touchpoint — not a long meeting — surfaces mindset issues and activity blockers before they become performance problems.
How to do it ▼
  • Keep it 5–10 minutes — a temperature check, not a full review
  • Cover two things: numbers (activity so far today) and mindset (how are they feeling?)
  • Reinforce early wins enthusiastically — confidence compounds fast in ramp
  • Adjust support based on the individual — not everyone ramps identically
  • Taper off after Day 90 as the rep hits their stride

Weekly Priorities

The two highest-impact items are 1:1s and forecast submission. If bandwidth is tight any given week, protect those two above all else.

🎯 Talent3 activities
1:1s with Each SD
Talent
"The 1:1 is where your coaching actually lands."
Why this matters
1:1s are your primary coaching surface — where Victory Plans get reinforced, personal blockers get surfaced, and reps feel seen. Without them you're managing output, not developing people.
How to do it ▼
  • Ask reps to come prepared: pipeline update, close-one wins to share, any blockers
  • Review Victory Plan progress — are they pacing to hit weekly commitments?
  • Celebrate close-one contributions explicitly — connect their work to bookings revenue
  • Create space for personal blockers: external factors impact performance too
  • End every 1:1 with one clear coaching action the rep commits to before next week
GongVictory Plan
Gong Call Coaching
Talent
"What you coach on Gong this week becomes a rep's habit next week."
Why this matters
Structured call review creates faster skill improvement than general feedback. Specific coaching on a specific call gives reps something concrete to apply immediately.
How to do it ▼
  • Review 1–2 calls per rep, or target reps with specific skill development needs
  • Focus coaching on the three core skills: UCPO, GCPI, and multi-threading
  • Use Hyperbound for targeted practice on gaps identified in Gong reviews
  • Provide feedback within 48 hours while the call is still fresh for the rep
  • Calibrate on business acumen and discovery quality — not just talk time or script adherence

Team Standup / Huddle
Talent
"Culture is built in the small moments, not the big meetings."
Why this matters
A weekly team touchpoint creates shared identity, keeps everyone aligned on priorities, and gives you a public forum to reinforce the revenue mindset. It's also where peer best practices spread most naturally.
How to do it ▼
  • Keep it short: 15–20 minutes — wins, blockers, this week's focus
  • Shout out close-one contributions and booking wins publicly — make revenue visible to the team
  • Rotate who shares a tip or insight each week — drives peer learning and ownership
  • Preview SPIFs, contests, or competitive moments to build early-quarter energy

⚡ Leverage3 activities
Submit Weekly Forecast
Leverage
"Forecasting is a skill. Treat it like one — and teach it to your reps."
Why this matters
Accurate forecasting demonstrates business acumen to leadership and models the revenue mindset for your team. It's one of the most underrated skills in the SD career path — and one of the most transferable to AE.
How to do it ▼
  • Lead with revenue in your forecast narrative — not just SQL counts
  • Include risk, upside, and key driver commentary — not just a number
  • Ask yourself: What will close? When? Why? If you can't answer, dig deeper first
  • Use the forecast call as a group coaching moment — model the revenue mindset publicly
  • Assess pipeline feasibility: are open opportunities genuinely progressing, or just aging?
Pipeline & Activity Review
Leverage
"Know where your softness lives before leadership does."
Why this matters
Weekly reviews let you catch deviations from the Victory Plan before they compound. The goal isn't surveillance — it's removing obstacles early so reps can perform at their best.
How to do it ▼
  • Use go/sdperf to review each rep's activity vs. their VP commitments
  • Use go/sdfunnel to identify where conversion breaks down: outreach volume, connect rate, or MQL→SAO
  • Intervene in real time for reps at risk — don't wait until end of week to flag it
  • Distinguish activity softness (volume) from quality softness (conversion) — the coaching fix is different
go/sdperf go/sdfunnel
Victory Plan Pacing Check
Leverage
"The VP is your true north — check you're still pointing at it."
Why this matters
The Victory Plan only works as an accountability tool if you actively reference it. A weekly check ensures reps are held to their own commitments — because they own the plan, not because you're enforcing it.
How to do it ▼
  • Reference the VP in every 1:1 — are weekly activity and pipeline targets on track?
  • Are we pacing against 10–11 active selling weeks this quarter — not 13?
  • If a rep is behind, recalibrate remaining weekly targets — don't assume they'll magically catch up
  • Use VP deviations as coaching conversations, not performance warnings

Monthly Priorities

Step back from daily execution to assess trends, develop people, and keep your talent pipeline healthy. These actions compound over the quarter.

⚡ Leverage2 activities
Monthly Attainment Analysis
Leverage
"Tell the story of the month — don't just report the number."
Why this matters
A data-backed narrative builds credibility with leadership and sharpens your strategic thinking. Monthly patterns reveal where your team consistently over- or under-performs and why.
How to do it ▼
  • Pull monthly attainment data vs. plan across SQL, SAO, and revenue contribution
  • Identify 1–2 key drivers: what went well and what held the team back?
  • Prepare a short narrative for leadership with risk, upside, and a plan to close any gaps
  • Look for seasonal patterns — month 1 of a quarter is typically slower for inbound; plan for it

SAO-to-SQO Conversion Review
Leverage
"Quality over quantity — but you need to measure it."
Why this matters
SAO → SQO is a leading indicator of pipeline quality. Sustained conversion below 80% typically signals a discovery or qualification problem — not just a lead volume issue.
How to do it ▼
  • Check team SAO → SQO conversion against the global benchmark of 80–85%
  • Identify reps below benchmark — is it a skills gap or a lead quality issue?
  • Review AE disqualification reasons: consistent patterns are a coaching signal, not a pipeline problem
  • For reps below 80%: build a targeted Gong review series focused on discovery quality

Milestone Check-ins for Ramping Reps
Talent
"A structured ramp produces consistent ramp times."
Why this matters
Formal milestone check-ins at 30, 60, and 90 days create mutual accountability and build a coaching record — over time revealing which ramp gaps predict future performance issues.
How to do it ▼
  • Day 30: All tools active, first call completed, product knowledge baseline, daily activity on target
  • Day 60: Pipeline building, first SAO submitted, Gong reviews underway, understanding AE workflow and SD's revenue contribution
  • Day 90: Pacing to quota, Victory Plan in place, demonstrating core skills (UCPO, GCPI, multi-threading)
  • Adjust support based on individual ramp pace — not everyone progresses identically
  • Document outcomes for each milestone to build your coaching record

Tool Adoption Audit
Talent
"A rep not using Gong is a rep you can't coach at scale."
Why this matters
Tool adoption directly limits coaching quality. If reps aren't using Gong and Hyperbound consistently, your ability to develop them is constrained. Low adoption is almost always a habit gap, not a motivation gap.
How to do it ▼
  • Check Gong call recording rates per rep — are all calls being captured?
  • Review Hyperbound session completion for reps on active coaching plans
  • For low adopters: make tool usage a Victory Plan expectation, not a suggestion
  • Stay current on tool updates — refresh team knowledge base monthly

Candidate Pipeline Review with TA
Talent
"Always be building your bench — not just filling open roles."
Why this matters
Reactive hiring creates bad hires. IB SD has high internal promotion rates — a success story that creates constant backfill pressure. A warm candidate pipeline means you're never scrambling when someone moves up.
How to do it ▼
  • Check in with your TA partner on pipeline health — are you pipelining ahead of need?
  • Share recent hire performance data to keep the ideal candidate profile calibrated
  • Key insight: 2+ years of prior SD experience is often a red flag — not a qualifier
  • Prioritize will-based hiring: look for self-reflection, resilience, and clear motivation over background
  • Non-traditional backgrounds (teachers, engineers, lawyers) consistently perform well in this role
Team Wins Celebration
Talent
"Recognition that comes late doesn't land."
Why this matters
Celebrating wins reinforces the behaviors you want repeated. Public recognition of close-one contributions connects individual effort to organizational impact — one of the highest-ROI things you do as a manager.
How to do it ▼
  • Celebrate close-one wins, booking milestones, and personal bests — not just quota attainment
  • Use available morale budget ($75/month) for team moments — don't hoard it until quarter end
  • Make recognition specific: "You got that bump because you multi-threaded early" lands better than "great job"
  • Explore SPIF opportunities to build competitive energy earlier in the quarter

Quarterly Priorities

The big levers. Many should be started 2–4 weeks before quarter end — not on Day 1 of the new quarter. These shape the trajectory of your team's performance and culture.

⚡ Leverage3 activities
Victory Planning for Next Quarter
Leverage
"Plan the quarter before it starts, not after."
Why this matters
Victory Plans created reactively in week 1 are already behind. Starting 2 weeks early means reps begin Day 1 executing — not planning. The VP is your primary accountability tool for the entire quarter.
How to do it ▼
  • Complete 2 weeks before quarter end — this is non-negotiable
  • Plan against 10–11 actual selling weeks, not 13–14 calendar weeks (account for PTO and holidays)
  • Assign individual revenue contribution numbers — every rep should know their personal bookings target
  • Break quota into weekly activity commitments — measurable progress every week, not just at quarter end
  • Build in seasonal pacing — don't assume a straight-line quota across each month
  • VP must cover: activity targets, pipeline targets (SQO + revenue), and the how
Victory Plan Template
QBR with AE Manager(s)
Leverage
"Your pipeline is a partnership. Treat it like one."
Why this matters
The SD–AE relationship is your most critical external partnership. Quarterly alignment on strategy, handoff quality, and deal slippage prevents the "great SAO, no bump" frustration that erodes trust on both sides.
How to do it ▼
  • Review last quarter: deal slippage, bump delays, multithreading gaps, AE feedback quality
  • Align on next quarter's strategy — priority segments, verticals, plays
  • Are AEs giving feedback in the system? Are SDs following up on aging opps? Address both sides
  • Discuss which tenured SDs are candidates for AE consideration
  • Agree on escalation norms: when should an SD handle a bump delay vs. loop you in?
Set Quarterly Pipeline & Revenue Targets
Leverage
"What's explicit gets executed. What's implied gets ignored."
Why this matters
Assigning individual revenue numbers to each rep changes the conversation from SQL counts to actual impact. The explicit target alone shifts how reps think about and talk about their work.
How to do it ▼
  • Set SQO and revenue pipeline targets per rep for the quarter
  • For SD2s: the revenue mindset target is 120% of assigned revenue quota — plan to overachieve
  • Communicate individual targets explicitly — every rep should articulate their personal bookings contribution
  • Plan for seasonal pacing — month 1 is typically slower for inbound; straight-line pacing sets reps up to feel behind from week 1
  • Participate actively in quota-setting discussions with leadership — you have the front-line context they need

Quarterly Team Reflection (Start / Stop / Continue)
Talent
"Reflection without structure is just venting."
Why this matters
Teams that reflect together improve together. A structured retrospective builds psychological safety, normalizes feedback culture, and surfaces team-level insights no 1:1 cadence can replicate.
How to do it ▼
  • Run this session early — don't wait until the last week of the quarter
  • Use the framework: What should we START? STOP? CONTINUE?
  • Celebrate wins first — set a positive tone before opening improvement discussions
  • Commit to 1–2 specific team-level changes coming out of each session
  • Customize format for your region or team culture — the framework is a guide, not a script
Succession Planning Review
Talent
"If you're constantly surprised by who leaves for AE, you're not planning."
Why this matters
IB SD has high internal promotion rates — a success story that creates constant backfill pressure. Proactive succession planning means you're never caught flat-footed when a top performer moves up.
How to do it ▼
  • Identify which reps are tracking toward IB→OB or OB→AE readiness this or next quarter
  • Use the AE/OB readiness checklist: hitting SQL quota + revenue target? Strong in UCPO, GCPI, multi-threading?
  • For AE track: have they demonstrated 5+ close-one experiences they can confidently articulate?
  • Empower tenured reps to own their pipeline with less daily oversight — this is part of their development
  • Alert your TA partner so backfill hiring can start proactively — not reactively
Hiring Calibration with TA
Talent
"Hiring well is the highest-leverage thing you do."
Why this matters
The ideal candidate profile evolves. Quarterly recalibration with TA — informed by how recent hires are actually performing — is how you continuously raise hiring quality rather than repeating past patterns.
How to do it ▼
  • Review this quarter's new hire performance data with your TA partner
  • Identify patterns: which backgrounds, experiences, and interview signals predicted success?
  • Update hiring criteria — share what the ideal profile is not, as well as what it is
  • Pressure-test AI fluency: candidates should demonstrate they can do more with AI, not just discuss it
  • Pressure-test will: ask how they rate their own performance and what they'd do differently

Annual Priorities

Build durably — your hiring infrastructure, promotion pipeline, coaching framework, and strategic positioning for the year ahead. These set the foundation everything else rests on.

🎯 Talent4 activities
Full Performance Reviews
Talent
"Evaluate the whole person — not just the number."
Why this matters
Annual reviews assess the three dimensions that predict long-term success: behavioral traits, skill development, and business acumen — not just whether someone hit quota.
How to do it ▼
  • Assess across three dimensions: behavioral (resilience, coachability), skill (UCPO, GCPI, multi-threading), and business acumen
  • Reference the full year of coaching documentation — not just recent performance
  • Use band scorecards as your calibration tool — apply consistently across the team
  • Ground every assessment in specific evidence: call examples, Gong reviews, VP data
  • Connect the review to a forward-looking development plan — not just a backwards-looking rating
Band ScorecardsGong
Promotion Criteria Refinement
Talent
"Promotion standards that don't evolve become irrelevant."
Why this matters
IB→OB and OB→AE criteria must reflect what's actually required for success in those next roles — and that changes as the business evolves. An annual review prevents promoting people into roles they aren't equipped for.
How to do it ▼
  • Review outcomes of all promotions made this year — are they succeeding in the next role?
  • Identify which criteria proved to be the strongest predictors of next-role success
  • Update the AE/OB readiness checklist accordingly — share across regions
  • Validate the 5+ close-one experiences bar for AE track is still the right standard
  • Refine what "strong in the three core skills" looks like as expectations evolve
Hiring Strategy for Next FY
Talent
"The best time to plan your next hire is before you need them."
Why this matters
Headcount planning at the start of FY sets you up for proactive bench building instead of reactive scrambling. Aligning with TA early means shaping the funnel before roles open up — not after.
How to do it ▼
  • Contribute to headcount planning discussions with Sales, SDOps, and Marketing
  • Align hiring criteria with TA for next FY — incorporate what this year's data taught you
  • Build AI fluency explicitly into the hiring profile — not as a bonus, as a baseline requirement
  • Agree on a bench-building target: how many qualified candidates should be in pipeline at any given time?
  • Commit to will-based hiring: energy, resilience, and growth mindset at the top of your criteria
Structured Coaching Framework Audit
Talent
"Are your coaching tools actually driving skill improvement?"
Why this matters
Coaching frameworks degrade without review. An annual audit of how you used band scorecards and Gong tells you whether your coaching has been consistent, effective, and genuinely moving rep performance.
How to do it ▼
  • Review band scorecard usage across all year-end reviews — was it applied consistently?
  • Audit Gong review frequency and coaching comment quality — are comments specific and actionable?
  • Identify which coaching interventions actually moved rep performance metrics
  • Share regional "secret sauce" practices with peer managers — cross-pollination raises the global floor
  • Update your personal coaching approach based on what the data shows actually worked
